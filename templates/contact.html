About me

My name is Bob Borsboom, 28 years old and live in Amsterdam. I live in a living project where I guide refugees with their first steps in The Netherlands. My task is to let them feel at home, help them learn the language, culture and make new friends. But I also learn a lot from them as they have a different culture, different kitchen and a different language.
Last year I graduated from the University of Amsterdam, with a master's degree in Artificial Intelligence. This was a technical master with a lot of mathematics, modeling, coding and fun. It also had much research as we discussed and implemented the latest models. I wrote my thesis at Aigritec, a company that tries to let robots take care of apple trees. This includes spraying the flowers and picking the apples during harvesting. My task was to write a depth estimation algorithm for the robot such that the robot could understand how far its objects are. More about my thesis can be found on the thesis page.

Bike trips & Volunteer work

In my free time, I love to do bike trips in Europe with voluntary work. I have done a bike trip to Barcelona and Slovenia. During these trips I did different kinds of volunteer work:
  • Working in a mountain hut in the Alps: building a wooden deck with other volunteers
  • Setting up a vegetable garden for a new community in Catalonia
  • Helping on a campsite/farm in France: Taking care of the animals, help during harvesting and welcoming camping guests
